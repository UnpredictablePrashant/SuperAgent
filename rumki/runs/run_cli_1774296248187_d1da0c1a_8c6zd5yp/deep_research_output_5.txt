2) Macro transmission: rates, inflation, USD, geopolitics
- Rates/real yields: Gold typically benefits from falling real yields; with the Fed steady in March and term yields near 4.2%, the path of policy easing and real yields remains the key macro “bridge” to bullion. WGC’s framework continues to highlight opportunity‑cost effects when policy expectations soften. ([kiplinger.com](https://www.kiplinger.com/investing/live/march-fed-meeting-2026-live-updates-and-commentary?utm_source=openai))
- Inflation expectations: CPI data for February were released March 11; inflation remains near mid‑2% y/y, keeping debate alive over how quickly real rates can compress. ([bls.gov](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_03112026.htm?utm_source=openai))
- U.S. dollar: The DXY rebounded toward ~100–101 in mid‑March, tempering gold rallies on some days; USD direction remains a near‑term swing factor.
